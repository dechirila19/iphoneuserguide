Introduction

Hello and welcome to the iPhone User Guide page. This page is dedicated to describing some of the apps the iPhone has and their features. We hope you find it useful!

Settings

The "Settings" app is one of the most important apps on your phone as you customize the settings according to your want or needs. Upon opening it, you will see the name of the iPhone with the Apple account that's signed into it. Click on your name and it'll show you more information like your subscriptions, payment method, iCloud backup and other devices using the same Apple account.

Back to the main settings page, you can choose from a whole bunch of settings to adjust. Examples include; Wifi Settings, Notifications, Mail App Settings... just to name a few. You can also check to see if there are any available software updates by going into the Settings app and checking right underneath your name. You can also find detailed informtion like your phones IMEI number in the Settings app.

App Store

You can download apps by going to the App Store on your phone. It is the blue icon with an "A" symbol.

Most apps are free but some you may need to purchase. At the bottom you will see the...

  • "Today" tab which will show things like the App of the day.
  • "Games" tab which will show you Game apps you can download and play.
  • "Apps" tab which will show you apps from all categories.
  • "Arcade" tab after it will show you arcade game apps you can download and play.
  • "Search" tab which you can use to search up any app that is available on the App Store.

    • Click on the silhouette icon at the top right. There you can see apps you've purchased/downloaded, see your subscriptions, redeem an App Store gift card and more.

    Messages

    Tap on the green icon with the white bubble... this is the Messages app. This is where you'll exchange texts with your contacts.

    Click on the upper right icon to start a new conversation. You can send emojis if you have the emoji keyboard activated by clicking on the smiley face in the lower left corner. You can also send photos by clicking on the white icon with the rainbow pinwheel. Your conversations will be blue and grey texts when texting other iPhone users/users using iMessage and green and grey when you're texting with someone who isn't using iPhone or iMessage.

    Safari

    Open the app with the compass icon... this is Safari, the web browser that comes with your phone.

    On the top bar is where the web address will be. You can click on this, type what you want to search and click enter to browse the web. At the bottom you will have your back button and forward button like on any browser. The middle button has many options such as sending the page to a friend through text or "favoriting" the page. The open book icon next to it has your history, reading list and bookmarks. The last button on the bottom all the way to your right are the open web pages you have.

    Camera

    Open the Camera app. It's hard to miss since it has the Camera icon. There is a lot packed in here. Lets start off with the top. On the top you'll have your "Flash" choice where you can turn flash on or off or leave it on auto. Next, the arrow in the middle changes the bottom bar from the different camera modes to another bar that has your choice of flash, LIVE photos, your timer, photo size and filters. Now back to the top bar, you'll have your "Live Photos" icon in the upper right which will let you choose if you want to take live photos or not.

    The bottom bar has all the different Camera modes. You can choose from Time-Lapse, Slo-Mo, Video, Photo, Portrait, and Pano (short for Panorama). Then on the lower left corner you'll have a small preview from the last photo you took. Click on it and you can scroll through the gallery of photos. The button underneath the bar on the bottom is your shutter button which you will click on to take a photo. The last icon to the right of it is the "Switch" button which allows you toggle between using the front-facing or back-facing camera.
